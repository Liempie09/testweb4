Fish and Chips

Britain's most iconic takeaway - crispy battered fish with golden chips

Historical Background

Fish and chips is one of the most famous British dishes. It started around the 1860s. The meal became popular in working-class areas in London and Northern England. Factory workers and their families liked it because it was cheap and filling. It is not clear where fish and chips first began. Some people say it started in London, others say it came from Lancashire. A man named Joseph Malin may have opened the first fish and chip shop in London. Another man, John Lees, sold fish and chips from a small hut in Lancashire around the same time. During World War II, fish and chips was very important in Britain. It was one of the few foods that was not rationed. The government believed it helped keep people happy and strong. Winston Churchill even gave the dish a special nickname. In the past, fish and chips were wrapped in newspaper to keep them warm. This is no longer allowed, but many people still remember it. Today, fish and chips is still very popular. There are thousands of fish and chip shops across the UK.

Cooking Instructions

1

Prepare the Chips

Peel the potatoes and cut into chips about 1cm thick. Rinse in cold water to remove excess starch, then pat dry thoroughly with kitchen paper.

2

First Fry the Chips

Heat oil to 130¬∞C (265¬∞F) in a deep fryer or large pan. Fry the chips in batches for 8-10 minutes until soft but not colored. Remove and drain on kitchen paper.

3

Make the Batter

In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Gradually whisk in the cold beer or sparkling water until you have a smooth, thick batter. Let it rest for 10 minutes.

4

Prepare the Fish

Pat the fish fillets dry with kitchen paper. Season with salt and pepper. Dust each fillet lightly with flour before dipping in batter.

5

Fry the Fish

Increase oil temperature to 180¬∞C (350¬∞F). Dip each fillet in batter, letting excess drip off. Carefully lower into the hot oil and fry for 6-8 minutes until golden and crispy. Drain on kitchen paper.

6

Second Fry the Chips

Return the chips to the oil at 180¬∞C and fry for 3-4 minutes until golden and crispy. Drain and season immediately with salt.

7

Serve

Serve the fish and chips immediately on warm plates or wrapped in paper. Add lemon wedges, mushy peas, and offer malt vinegar and tartar sauce on the side. Enjoy your authentic British fish and chips!

Tips & Variations

Temperature Control

Use a thermometer to maintain proper oil temperature. Too hot and the batter burns; too cool and it becomes greasy.

üêü

Fish Selection

Cod and haddock are traditional, but you can also use plaice, pollock, or even sustainable alternatives like coley.

üç∫

Batter Secret

Using cold beer or sparkling water creates a lighter, crispier batter. The carbonation helps create air pockets when frying.

ü•î

Perfect Chips

Double frying is essential! The first fry cooks the potato, the second creates the crispy exterior. Don't skip this step.

üßÇ

Seasoning Timing

Always season chips immediately after frying while they're still hot. The salt will stick better and distribute evenly.

ü´õ

Mushy Peas

Make authentic mushy peas by simmering dried marrowfat peas with a pinch of sugar and butter until soft and creamy.

My homemade Fish and Chips

Making My First Fish and Chips

I made fish and chips by following a recipe from the British Kitchen website. The dish in the photo shows the final result that I prepared myself.

First, I prepared the potatoes. I peeled the potatoes and cut them into thick chips. I washed them in cold water and dried them with kitchen paper. I fried the chips in hot oil for the first time until they were soft but not brown. Then I took them out and let them rest. Next, I made the batter. In a bowl, I mixed flour, baking powder, salt, and cold beer (or water). I mixed everything until the batter was smooth.

After that, I prepared the fish. I dried the fish fillets with kitchen paper and added salt and pepper. I covered the fish lightly with flour and dipped it into the batter. Then, I heated the oil to a higher temperature and fried the fish. I fried it until it was golden and crispy. When it was ready, I took it out and placed it on kitchen paper.

After that, I fried the chips a second time until they were golden and crispy. I added salt to the chips. Finally, I served the fish and chips on a plate like in the photo. I added lemon wedges and tartar sauce. The kitchen was a bit messy, but the fish and chips tasted very good.
